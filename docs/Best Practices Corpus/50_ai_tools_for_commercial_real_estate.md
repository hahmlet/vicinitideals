## Natural Language Models and Interfaces (Open-Weight)

Open‑weight models are self‑hostable or broadly licensed models whose weights you can download, fine‑tune, and deploy on your own infrastructure, while still accessing many via managed APIs for convenience. This section focuses on families that matter for real work, not just benchmarks.

To access a wide-range of open-weight models, check out [huggingface.co](http://huggingface.co/).

#### DeepSeek

DeepSeek (V3 and V3.2 families) is an open‑weight large language model line focused on frontier‑level reasoning with high efficiency, targeting math, coding, and agentic workflows while remaining practical to self‑host.

- V3.2 (released December 2025) remains DeepSeek's current flagship, introducing DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA), a fine‑grained sparse attention mechanism that substantially improves long‑context efficiency while preserving model quality. It performs comparably to GPT‑5 on key benchmarks, with the V3.2‑Speciale variant surpassing GPT‑5 on hard reasoning tasks.
- V3.2‑Speciale achieved gold‑medal‑level performance on IMO 2025, IOI 2025, CMO 2025, and ICPC World Finals - matching the reasoning proficiency of Gemini 3.0 Pro. Both models are open-sourced under the MIT license on Hugging Face and ModelScope.
- "Thinking in Tool‑Use" allows DeepSeek‑V3.2 to integrate internal reasoning directly into tool calls, supporting both thinking and non‑thinking modes for complex, tool‑heavy agent workflows.
- DeepSeek V4, described as a native multimodal model with a 1M+ token context window and enhanced coding capabilities, is anticipated for release in April 2026 but has not yet officially launched as of this writing.

#### Qwen (Qwen3.5 / Qwen3.6 Family - Alibaba)

Alibaba's Qwen family has advanced rapidly through Qwen3, Qwen3.5, and now Qwen3.6 in early 2026, delivering native multimodal capabilities, efficient hybrid architectures, and broad multilingual coverage targeting enterprise agentic workflows across cloud and on‑prem deployments.

- Qwen3.5 (released February 16, 2026) is a 397B-parameter open-weight Mixture of Experts model activating ~17B parameters per token, combining standard attention with linear attention heads and gated delta networks for hardware-efficient inference. It supports 201 languages and dialects (up from 82 in prior generations) and includes native multimodal capabilities for text, images, and video.
- Smaller open-weight variants - Qwen3.5-122B, 35B, 27B, 9B, and 4B - were released through February and March 2026, making the family accessible from edge devices to enterprise servers. All are available under open licenses on Hugging Face and ModelScope.
- Qwen3.5-Omni (March 30, 2026) is a native omni-modal model using a Thinker-Talker architecture to process text, images, audio, and video in a single pipeline, supporting 256K context, 10+ hours of audio, speech recognition in 113 languages, and real-time voice interaction with semantic interruption.
- Qwen3.6-Plus (April 2, 2026), the latest proprietary flagship, focuses on agentic coding with a 1M-token context window by default, improved front-end generation and code debugging, and is available via Alibaba Cloud's Bailian platform and compatible with third-party tools including OpenClaw, Claude Code, and Cline.

#### Kimi (Kimi K2.5 - Moonshot)

Kimi K2.5 is Moonshot's flagship open‑source, native multimodal agentic model, built through continual pretraining on roughly 15 trillion mixed visual and text tokens to deliver strong reasoning, coding, and vision‑language capabilities.

- K2.5 is a Mixture‑of‑Experts model with about 1T total parameters and 32B active parameters per token, using Multi‑head Latent Attention (MLA), SwiGLU activations, and a MoonViT vision encoder.
- The model natively supports images, video (experimental), PDFs, and text as inputs, enabling rich multimodal understanding and generation for documents, UI designs, and visual workflows.
- K2.5 is optimized for "agentic" use, supporting instant and thinking modes, swarm‑style task decomposition, and tool use grounded in visual inputs for complex, multi‑step workflows.
- Open‑weight checkpoints are published on major hubs and integrated into platforms like NVIDIA NIM and Hugging Face, making K2.5 accessible for self‑hosting and enterprise deployments.
